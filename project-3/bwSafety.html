Staying Safe Online

Safety is not about “being strong enough” to ignore harm. It is about having tools, support, and language to protect yourself and others when something feels wrong in a chat, group, or game.

Trust Your Unease

If a message makes you feel scared, guilty, pressured, or confused, that feeling matters. You do not need proof that someone is “bad” in order to step back, block, or ask for help. Discomfort is already information.

Keep Your Circle

Staying connected to friends, family, counselors, or other trusted people is one of the strongest protections against manipulation. Anyone who demands total secrecy or tries to cut you off from others is not safe.

Pause Before Responding

You are allowed to take time. You can close the app, mute notifications, or say, “I need a break.” Urgency and “answer now or else” messages are often used to push past your boundaries.

Save Evidence

Screenshots and message logs can help if you decide to report someone or talk to a trusted adult or support service. Keeping records is not “dramatic”; it is a form of self-protection and documentation.

Block & Report

Using block, mute, and report tools is a valid response to harm. Platforms have a responsibility to act, and you are never obligated to keep engaging with someone who is making you feel unsafe or trapped.

You Deserve Support

Reaching out for help is not a failure; it is a form of resistance. Whether it is a friend, teacher, family member, or helpline, you deserve to be taken seriously when something online is hurting you or someone you care about.
