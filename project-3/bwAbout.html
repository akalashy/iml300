About This Project

Beyond the Blue Whale Game is an interactive website that examines the digital myths, manipulations, and social fears surrounding the Blue Whale challenge. While the moral panic around the challenge was often exaggerated, the vulnerability of young users in online spaces is real. This project explores how harmful narratives spread, how isolation and secrecy are used as tools of manipulation, and how digital environments can shape mental health and self-perception.

I created this website for teenagers, educators, parents, and anyone navigating the blurred space between online influence and personal safety. By combining research, media analysis, and interactive elements, the project aims to reveal how dangerous online behaviors are not caused by a “game,” but by systemic issues: lack of digital literacy, emotional isolation, and exploitative online dynamics.

This work responds to social justice issues by emphasizing youth protection, mental health awareness, and the importance of accessible digital education. Instead of sensationalizing fear, the project encourages critical thinking, informed choices, and safer online community practices.
